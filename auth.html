Authentication

The purpose of authentication is to prove who you really are to gain access to certain data. This establishes trust between the two parties, enabling the sharing of information. There are 3 main ways to authenticate: something you know, have, or are.

Something You Know

     This is also known as a "Knowledge Factor" as it is something the person who is attempting to access the information already knows. A basic example of this is a password you created for an app or account. Another example of this is a security question which you set when creating an account. However, these factors are all low security as a hacker can easily steal, crack, or guess them.

Source: Thrive Digital

Source: Malwarebytes

Something You Have

     This factor is also known as a "Possession Factor" since it requires the possession of a physical item. An example of a possession factor is a mobile phone, smart card, or hardware token. An authenticator app on your phone which displays a code for your login is an example of this. The possession factor is more secure than the knowledge factor since it requires hackers to gain remote access to hardware or steal that hardware.

Something you Are

     Lastly, the "Inherence Factor" is something that requires evidence that is inherent to a user's unique features. Because of this, the Inherence Factor is the strongest of all authentication methods. An example of this is the use of biometrics, such as a fingerprint scan or facial recognition.
