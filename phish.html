Phishing

Phishing is used in a majority of cyber attacks done to gain confidential information about a target. It is one of the top 4 biggest threats in the digital world.

What is Phishing?

     A phishing attack is when a malicious actor utilizes fake emails or websites to trick users into entering passwords or other personal information. An example of a phishing attack is an email that appears to be from a company you know, but in reality it directs you to a fake webpage that will steal your information. These attacks are often very convincing and lead to millions of people falling for them.

How do you avoid Phishing attacks?

     Phishing attacks are usually very hard to notice, but a few key details can make a big difference. Any misspellings or incorect grammer is often a way to differentiate a fake email from a real one. If the sender and email address do not match, the email is likely to be a phishing email. Also, hovering over URLs before clicking on them is recommended as it might not always match the text, giving away the trap.

Other types of phishing

     There are many other types of phishing which one might encounter in the cyberspace. Vishing is an attack where fake phone calls or voice messages are used to trick users into giving personal information to malicious actors. Along with this, smishing is a type of phishing which uses text messages instead of emails. It is important to report a phishing attack anytime you see one so that other do not fall for the same scam.
