Potential Threats

There are many potential threats which one might face during their time in the cyberspace. Here are some examples of these threats and ways to avoid them.

Source: Avast

DDoS Attacks

     A DDos attack is an attack which overwhelms a service or system to the point where it is unable to respond to computers trynig to access it. This attack floods a website or server with malicious traffic and makes it unable to operate as it normally would. If you run servers that might be hit with this type of attack, making sure that your network is resilient and having a plan for this type of attack is important.

Ransomware

     Ransomware is the act of holding a victim's system hostage untill a ransom is payed to the attacker. After paying the ransom, the attacker gives the user instructions on how to regain control of their computer. The ransomware is usually downloaded unintentionally from a website or email, so one should double check the email and website that they are opening before they go through with any type of download.

Source: Cybeready

Source: Thrive DX

Trojan Horses

     A trojan horse attack is a malicious program that is hidden under the guise of a legitimage one. Similar to the phishing attack, a malicious actor uses the cover of a legitimate application or program to disguise the virus or backdoor. Using this trojan, a hacker can take control of a user's computer and acecss the network it is connected to. To avoid this attack, one should use software to check what they are downloading is legitimate and not something dangerous.
