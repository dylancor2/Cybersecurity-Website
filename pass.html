Passwords

Although creating a password might seem straight forward, making a strong password that prevents hackers from accessing information is necessary and important to stay safe online.

Source: SSBWYO

Making a Strong Password

     A strong password containes at least 12 characters, but the longer the better. It should also contain a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, with some numbers and symbols. The password should avoid words from the dictionary and be different from any of your previous passwords.

Securing Your Password

     Make sure that you never share your passwords with anyone. This is a potential way that your account can be compromised, so one should be careful with who they tell their passwords to. You should also never send a password by email or instant message since these methods of communication are not reliably secure. Using unique passwords and a password manager is recommended to secure all acounts and information.

Source: LPP

Source: PC Mag

A Hacker's Point of View

     Hackers have multiple tools when it comes to breaking through passwords. A hacker might use a keylogger or recording of a user putting in their password to access accounts. They might also use Brute Force to enter hundreds of passwords every second to find the correct one. Database leaks and breaches also allow hackers to find confidential information, so it is important to change your password after a service you have used has a data leak.
